CRYPTOGRAPHIC FOUNDATION

Quantum-resistant from
genesis block zero.

Two NIST-standardised post-quantum algorithms secure every account, transaction and block — not retrofitted later, but present since block zero. Migrating an existing chain to post-quantum cryptography means redesigning consensus, storage and fees from scratch. Shamwari already did that work, which is why waiting for competitors to catch up is not a strategy.

ML-DSA: Dilithium
NIST FIPS 204
Every transaction, block and certificate is signed with lattice-based cryptography that stays secure even against a large-scale quantum computer running Shor's algorithm — the attack that breaks ECDSA and RSA. Deployed since block zero, so there's no history left exposed.
ML-KEM: Kyber
NIST FIPS 203
Encrypts account identity, KYC records, messages and payment payloads before they ever touch the chain, using hybrid key encapsulation plus AES-256-GCM — so sensitive data stays private even from an attacker recording today's traffic to decrypt once quantum computers arrive.

Why Shamwari?

Four structural problems in existing financial infrastructure. Four protocol-layer solutions built into Shamwari from genesis.

WHY EXISTING SYSTEMS FAIL
1
No protocol-native identity
Banks require government-issued ID and a fixed address. 1.1 billion people globally have no legal identity. Without identity there is no account. Without an account there is no access to savings, credit or remittances.
2
Remittance corridors extract 8–12%
Sub-Saharan Africa is the world's most expensive remittance corridor. $48B in fees annually is extracted from the people who can least afford it, before the money ever reaches their families.
3
Credit is unavailable without credit history
MFIs rely on informal credit assessment and high operational costs. No on-chain credit record, no automated underwriting, no collateral mechanism that works without a bank account. Informal lenders charge 40–200% APR.
4
Smart-contract chains don't solve it
Generic blockchains bring gas fees, wallet complexity, and compliance that lives in application code, not the protocol. A DeFi app is not a substitute for regulated financial infrastructure — especially not for someone earning a few dollars a day on a basic phone.
HOW SHAMWARI RESPONDS
BARRIER 1: IDENTITY
Protocol-native typed identity
Account types built around how institutions are actually regulated, with KYC data encrypted on-chain using post-quantum cryptography. Regulator-issued digital certificates replace paper-based KYC — accounts open from any internet-enabled device, with no branch visit, no paperwork, and no minimum deposit.
BARRIER 2: REMITTANCES
Built-in DEX with instant FX settlement
ShamwariPay settles cross-border transfers directly between jurisdiction chains through a built-in exchange — confirmed in under a minute, with no correspondent bank, no SWIFT, and no arbitrary corridor fee. Tax is withheld automatically at settlement.
BARRIER 3: CREDIT
On-chain credit history and P2P lending
Every repayment, default and collateral event becomes an immutable on-chain record — the first verifiable credit history available to people outside the banking system. MFI accounts get automated credit scoring and the full loan lifecycle, with collateral enforced by the protocol.
BARRIER 4: ARCHITECTURE
A financial protocol, not a general-purpose chain
Every financial primitive — payments, exchange, lending, tax, identity — is enforced by consensus, not smart contract code. No gas fees pricing out small transactions. And unlike chains adapted for finance after the fact, Shamwari has been quantum-safe since genesis block zero.
